Overview

In order to replicate (somewhat) realistic rocket-physics, it was necessary to implement a control scheme that governs the speed of the rocket's actuators. The speed of the x and y axes of the rocket gantry is determined based on a simple kinematic model where two forces act on the rocket: gravity and thrust. Thrust is applied downward along the rocket's longitudinal axis, and gravity acts in the negative y-axis.

The motor's speed is proportional to a PWM duty cycle. We characterized the deadband of the motor by controlling and monitoring its speed over vendor requests.
